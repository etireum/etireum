Ethereum Official Site ↗

Comprehensive introduction to Ethereum, covering its purpose and functionalities.

Ethereum is a decentralized, open-source blockchain platform that enables smart contract functionality and supports the creation of decentralized applications (dApps) through its native Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM). Utilizing a proof-of-stake consensus mechanism since the 2022 Merge, the network allows developers to build and deploy autonomous, programmable smart contracts that execute predefined actions when specific conditions are met. The platform's native cryptocurrency, Ether (ETH), serves as both a transactional currency and a critical component for executing computational operations, with gas fees paid in ETH to compensate network validators for processing and verifying transactions. By providing a robust, flexible infrastructure for blockchain development, Ethereum has become a foundational technology for numerous decentralized finance (DeFi), non-fungible token (NFT), and Web3 applications across various industries.

Ethereum Account Abstraction ↗

Introduction to Ethereum’s new account model that improves security and usability.

Ethereum Account Abstraction (ERC-4337) introduces a novel approach to blockchain account management by enabling smart contract wallets that can define custom authentication and transaction execution logic. Unlike traditional externally owned accounts (EOAs), these programmable wallets can implement advanced features such as multi-factor authentication, social recovery, and gas payment flexibility through custom validation rules. The standard introduces a new mempool and entry point contract that allows users to initiate transactions through UserOperations, which can be bundled and processed by specialized network participants called bundlers. By decoupling authentication and transaction execution from the underlying blockchain primitive, Account Abstraction significantly enhances user experience and wallet programmability while maintaining core Ethereum security principles.

Ethereum Roadmap ↗

Ethereum’s official roadmap clearly laying out upcoming upgrades and developments.

The Ethereum Roadmap represents a comprehensive, multi-phase strategy to enhance the network's scalability, security, and sustainability through a series of interconnected upgrades. Key phases include the Merge (completed in September 2022), which transitioned Ethereum from Proof-of-Work to Proof-of-Stake, and subsequent stages focusing on sharding, rollups, and layer-2 solutions to dramatically increase transaction throughput and reduce network congestion. The roadmap aims to implement proto-danksharding and full danksharding to enable more efficient data availability, reduce transaction costs, and support a more decentralized ecosystem of layer-2 networks and rollups. By incrementally addressing technical challenges through rigorous research and community-driven development, the Ethereum Roadmap seeks to evolve the network into a more robust, scalable, and sustainable blockchain infrastructure capable of supporting complex decentralized applications and global economic transactions.

Ethereum EIPs ↗

Ethereum Improvement Proposals

Ethereum Improvement Proposals (EIPs) are design documents providing information to the Ethereum community, describing new features or processes for the Ethereum platform. They serve as the primary mechanism for proposing changes to the Ethereum protocol, documenting design decisions, and ensuring community consensus. Each EIP is a standalone document that includes a concise technical specification of the feature and a rationale for the feature. EIPs are categorized into standards track, informational, and meta, with standards track EIPs further divided into core, networking, interface, and ERC (Ethereum Request for Comments) subcategories.

MEV-Boost ↗

Proposer-builder separation for Ethereum

MEV-Boost is a middleware solution designed to optimize block building and maximize validator rewards in proof-of-stake Ethereum networks by enabling validators to outsource block construction to specialized block builders. It implements a market-driven approach where block builders compete to create the most profitable blocks, leveraging sophisticated strategies to extract maximum value through techniques like transaction reordering and arbitrage. The protocol introduces a separation of concerns between block proposal and block construction, allowing validators to access a more diverse and potentially more lucrative set of blocks compared to local block building methods. By implementing a standardized relayer architecture, MEV-Boost facilitates a more efficient and transparent mechanism for managing maximal extractable value (MEV) while maintaining the core principles of decentralization and network integrity.

What is a mempool. ↗

A blog post by Alchemy to explain what the mempool is.

A mempool (memory pool) is a critical component of blockchain networks where unconfirmed transactions are temporarily stored and validated before being added to a block. Nodes in the network maintain their own mempools, which act as staging areas for transactions that have been broadcast but not yet included in a confirmed block, allowing miners or validators to select and prioritize transactions based on factors like transaction fees and network congestion. These pending transactions remain in the mempool until they are either successfully mined into a block or discarded due to various reasons such as low fees, conflicting transactions, or network rules. The mempool serves as a crucial mechanism for managing transaction flow, ensuring network efficiency, and providing visibility into pending blockchain activities.

Lens Protocol ↗

Decentralized Social Media on Ethereum

Lens Protocol is a blockchain-based, decentralized social graph built on Polygon that enables users to truly own their social media content and connections through NFT-based profiles. Unlike traditional social platforms, Lens allows creators to port their entire social network, followers, and content across different applications while maintaining full data sovereignty and monetization control. The protocol utilizes a modular architecture that permits developers to build diverse social media experiences on top of a shared, interoperable backend, with each user's profile represented as a non-transferable NFT that acts as a portable identity. By leveraging Web3 principles, Lens Protocol fundamentally reimagines social media infrastructure, providing composable, permissionless social interactions where users have complete ownership and programmable rights over their digital social presence.
